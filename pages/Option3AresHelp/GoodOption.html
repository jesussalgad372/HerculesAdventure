The Iron-Fisted Victor

With Ares' direct, unholy blessing, Hercules becomes an unstoppable engine of war.
Troy falls in a matter of days, the city walls crumbling under a power fueled by divine wrath.
Hercules's sheer, overwhelming might and the terror he inspires in his enemies end the war quickly, saving countless Greek lives.
The other gods are horrified and disgusted by the bloodshed, but cannot deny the decisive victory.
Hercules returns a conqueror, but not a hero.
He rules over a new, powerful kingdom, but it is built on fear, not respect.
He is a king of immense power, but his reign is cold and brutal, and he and his kingdom are forever associated with the slaughter of Troy.
