The Hallow Victory

Ares grants Hercules his strength, and the Trojan War is won with a terrifying speed and efficiency.
But the cost is immense.
Ares demands an exorbitant price for his divine aid, extracting tribute from Hercules and his followers for years to come.
The war's end brings no joy, only a crushing sense of emptiness and loss.
The Greeks return victorious but are morally and spiritually wounded by the level of violence and destruction.
Hercules, though powerful and respected, is forever changed by his deal with the god of war.
He is a living testament to the truth that some victories are not worth the price paid.
His fame is great, but it is a bitter and somber fame, and he finds no true peace or happiness in his remaining years.
